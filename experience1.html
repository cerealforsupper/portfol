SWRAP @ STMicroelectronics

I had the opportunity to go for a 4 day work attachment program at STMicroelectronic's AMK plant to learn more about the production and manufacturing of semiconductors. This was done through the SWRAP program (Structured Work Reasearch & Attachment Programme).

Day 1 | Introduction

Us students were given a tour around the STMicroelectronics facility to familiarise ourselves with the place. We also had a brief introduction session to the company to learn more about what STMicroelectronics do.

Day 2 | Job Shadowing

As part of the work attachment program, I was assigned to a mentor for job shadowing. I was brought to the filter and abatement section where I learnt how waste produced by the production of wafers for chips are sustainbly dealt with.

Day 3 | Fab Lab Tour & Product Development

We were given the rare opportunity to actually enter and visit a wafer fabrication lab where I gained a deeper insight into the manufacturing of wafers for semiconductors. The entire section was very uncontaminated as small dust particles would significantly affect wafer production in the Lab.

We also had the chance to meet the product development team where they presented on how products of the company go from the drawing board all the way into the market.

Day 4 | Conclusion

To end off things, we presented on our journey through the previous 3 days infront of the mentors and staff that had guided us through.

This experience has taught me that many of the technologies such as semiconductors found in our everyday devices are not easily made and should not be taken for granted. I also gained much insight on other sections such as what it is like to work in the semiconductor industry and sustainable ethics in company actions.
